Directed by Francis Ford Coppola: "The Godfather" is a cinematic masterpiece that tells the epic story of the Corleone crime family. It explores themes of power, family, and morality as the patriarch, Vito Corleone (Marlon Brando), navigates the world of organized crime. With its iconic performances, intricate storytelling, and unforgettable dialogue, it's a timeless classic that redefined the gangster genre.
